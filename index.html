CHICKEN KATSU

Torikatsu – Crispy Japanese Chicken Cutlet

↓

Origin

Chicken Katsu, also known as Torikatsu, is a Japanese dish made from breaded and deep-fried chicken cutlet. It is inspired by the traditional pork version called Tonkatsu.

Tonkatsu first appeared in Japan in the late 19th century during the Meiji period, when Western cooking techniques influenced Japanese cuisine. Over time, chicken became a popular alternative because it is leaner and cooks faster than pork.

The word "katsu" comes from the Japanese word katsuretsu, which means cutlet.

Cultural Significance

🥪

Katsu Sando

Chicken katsu is commonly used in the famous Japanese sandwich called Katsu Sando. It is served with soft bread, cabbage, and tonkatsu sauce.

🍋

Serving Style

Chicken Katsu is typically served with steamed rice, shredded cabbage, and tonkatsu sauce. Some restaurants also add a lemon wedge for extra flavor.

🍛

Katsu Curry

Chicken Katsu is also popular in Japanese curry dishes where crispy chicken cutlet is served with thick Japanese curry sauce and rice.

Classic Chicken Katsu Recipe

Ingredients

Chicken Breast
All-Purpose Flour
Eggs
Panko Breadcrumbs
Vegetable Oil
Salt & Pepper
Lemon Wedges

Cooking Steps

1. Prepare Chicken

Butterfly the chicken breast and pound it gently until evenly thin.

2. Coat the Chicken

Dredge the chicken in flour, dip in beaten egg, and coat with panko breadcrumbs.

3. Fry

Heat oil to about 175°C (350°F). Fry the chicken for 3–4 minutes per side until golden brown and cooked through.

4. Serve

Slice the chicken and serve with rice, cabbage, and tonkatsu sauce.
